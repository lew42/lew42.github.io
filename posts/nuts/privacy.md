# Info Privacy

- SSN?
- Passwords?
- Biometrics (DNA)

Right now, our current state of affairs is terrible:
1)  No password for our social security number
2)  Access to our email is considered authorization by most services (if someone can access your email, you're pretty much screwed).
3)  We, as humans, have to reuse passwords.  We can't memorize unique 32 character passwords for every site.  And so, if we sign up for service with our email and password, hackers can try to use those login credentials on any other site.

## Is encryption good?

Some privacy is acceptable.  But there should always be a backdoor?  It's a very fine line.  You can only implement self-survellience when you have 100% confidence in your system.
